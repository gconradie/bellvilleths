The Tradition and History of Bellville Technical High School

Vision and Mission

Building my Future

• To be the best technical school in South Africa through dedication, self-discipline, leadership and accountability of the staff and learners.

• To prepare learners (through a streamlined curriculum) for vocational advancement and tertiary levels, for the needs of the corporate world, and by commiting to constant innovation and best practises in the 21st century.

• The motto of Bellville Technical High School (BTHS) is, in the fullest sense, also its major goal: to produce a pupil fully-equipped to face the future challenges of the technological profession. This entails professional instruction at the highest levels in the three major areas; namely the sciences, humanities and technology.

Our Code of Honour

BELIEF

  1. Bravery/Courage - Be courageous. Stand up for your principles. Don’t do the popular thing. Do what is right.
  2. Endurance - Happy is the person who remains steadfast under trial, for having passed that test he will receive for his prize the gift of life.   
  3. Loyalty/Love - Loyal to yourself, your family, your school and your Creator.

  4. Integrity - Adhering to your moral principles. Virtues like honour, honesty, respect, dignity and responsibility.
  5. Exemplary - To set the example regarding virtues at all times.

  6. Fairness - To expect to be treated with fairness and to be fair to others

The School Badge

Scientia et Sollertia - Knowledge and Skill

The motif represented by the associated bearings can be related to the principle at which a regulator functions.

A regulator is used to create co-operative dynamics within various technological fields. A gyroscope, which indicates stability in all circumstances, also functions on this principle.   The motif can also indicate an atom - a powerful source of energy.

The three bearings also symbolize the ideal educational relationship between learner, parent and educator.

The motif also indicates through its three interconnected lines, the inner dependence of the three core facets wherein the school operates namely, the sciences, humanities and technology.


The three bearings also represent the three facts in technical education which are interalia, development, manual skills and spiritual development.
